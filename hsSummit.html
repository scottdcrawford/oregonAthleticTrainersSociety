OATS High School Summit

This event has been rescheduled


Eugene, OR. - The Oregon Athletic Trainers’ Society (OATS) will be hosting its High School Summit February 18, 2017. The Summit is designed to provide continuing education specific to the high school athletic trainer. This year’s event will take place at Sheldon High School in the Library.


The topics for this year’s Summit feature two talks on return to learn following concussion. Melissa McCart, from the Center on Brain Injury Research and Training at the University of Oregon, will discuss classroom accommodations following concussions. Winston Cornwall, from the Oregon Department of Education, will present on 504 plans and how it relates to student-athletes with concussions. High School athletic trainers are being called upon to be members of the school’s concussion management team and these talks will provide the athletic trainer with the necessary background for this role.

The Summit will also focus on policy and procedure development for the high school athletic trainer. Scott Neufeld and Jens Jensen from Property and Casualty Coverage for Education, which is the leading liability insurance carrier for Oregon schools, will present on what policies and procedures are needed for high school athletic trainers. This will be followed by an open roundtable for athletic trainers in attendance to share ideas how they have developed policies and procedures. Athletic trainers are encouraged to bring their policies and procedures manual to work and share with others. Considering that all athletic trainers in the high school setting need policies and procedures in place, these sessions will help provide the jump start to develop them or provide an opportunity to refine existing ones.

The day will close with a hands-on session on spine boarding. With proposed changes to the spine boarding guidelines it is important that all athletic trainers become familiar with the new recommendations. Further, the fact that high school athletic trainers are often in facilities without any other athletic trainers it is difficult to practice these skills on a regular basis.

“I believe the OATS Secondary School Committee has done an outstanding job putting together an excellent agenda for the High School Summit,” OATS President Sam Johnson said. “The topics they chose are important issues for all athletic trainers, but especially those in the high school setting. I look forward to seeing how these talks can help the profession grow.”

Athletic trainers can receive up to 6 Category A CEUs for attending. Athletic trainers are also encouraged to invite other members of their Concussion Management Team to sit in for the Concussion Management sessions at no charge (no CEUs will be awarded if the team member is not an athletic trainer).

The cost for athletic trainers is $25 if registered by December 15th and $35 after December 15th. Student can attend the event for $5 if registered before the 15th and $10 after the 15th. Online registration and the agenda are available here.

Please contact Marlee Hansen, OATS Secondary Schools Committee Chair, at brownlie@4j.lane.edu with any questions.
